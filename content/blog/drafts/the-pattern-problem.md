### Two types of data journalism

1. **Illustrative**: Data supports a story you already know is true
2. **Investigative**: Data reveals something no one knew

Most newsrooms do #1 and call it #2.

### What I learned from the NYPD project

Built a network of 29,915 officers. Found "catalyst" clusters.

The scary part: the math could have been wrong. The only way to know was to have George Joseph interview victims and see if their stories matched the network structure.

They did. But we didn't know that until we checked.

### The validation loop

1. Find pattern in data
2. Generate hypothesis
3. Test hypothesis with traditional reporting
4. Only publish if reporting confirms

Most data journalism skips step 3.

### Why I document methodology obsessively

If another city wants to replicate this analysis, they can. That's the point.
